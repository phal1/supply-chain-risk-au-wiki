# OAIC — Office of the Australian Information Commissioner

## Overview

The OAIC is the independent regulator for privacy and freedom of information in Australia. It administers the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), the Freedom of Information Act 1982, and the My Health Records Act 2012.

## Role in Supply Chain / TPRM

OAIC's key contribution to TPRM is the **Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) scheme**, which requires organisations to notify the OAIC and affected individuals when an eligible data breach occurs. For supply chains:

- Entities remain responsible for data breaches occurring through **third-party service providers**
- Due diligence on service providers' data handling is explicitly expected
- Fourth-party (sub-processor) risks must be considered

## Notifiable Data Breaches Statistics

- H2 2024: 595 breaches reported (15% increase)
- H1 2025: 532 breaches reported (10% decrease)
- ~67% caused by malicious/criminal attacks
- Top sectors: health, finance, government, retail

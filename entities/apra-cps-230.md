# APRA CPS 230 — Operational Risk Management

## Overview

[[apra]] Prudential Standard CPS 230 consolidates five prior standards (CPS 231, CPS 232, SPS 231, SPS 232, HPS 231) into a single operational risk and business continuity framework. Effective **1 July 2025** for all APRA-regulated entities.

## Key Requirements

### Service Provider Management
- Board-approved service provider management policy
- Critical service provider identification and ongoing monitoring
- Contractual exit/transition provisions
- Pre-contractual risk assessments

### Business Continuity
- Critical operations identified (payments, clearing, custody for banks; claims for insurers)
- Tolerance levels: max tolerable disruption period, max data loss, min service levels
- BCP testing with severe but plausible scenarios

### Operational Risk Framework
- Operational risk profile assessment
- Risk appetite with indicators and limits
- Internal controls effectiveness monitoring

## Third Party Risk Implications

CPS 230's service provider requirements are the most directly applicable TPRM regulation in Australia. Key implications:
- Third parties performing critical operations must meet same resilience standards as internal teams
- Fourth-party (sub-processor) risks must be assessed
- Pre-existing contracts must be remediated by earlier of next renewal or 1 July 2026

## Enforcement

APRA has broad intervention powers including requiring additional capital, imposing licence conditions, or requiring independent remediation.

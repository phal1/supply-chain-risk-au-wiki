# Privacy Act 1988 (Cth)

## Overview

The Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) governs the collection, use, storage, and disclosure of personal information in Australia. It includes the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) and the Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) scheme.

## Relevance to Supply Chain / TPRM

Key provisions affecting third-party risk:

### APP 3 — Collection of Personal Information
- Requires entities to notify individuals of third parties to whom data may be disclosed

### APP 5 — Notification of Collection
- Must disclose if personal information will be collected from third parties

### APP 11 — Security of Personal Information
- Requires "reasonable steps" to protect personal information from misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorised access
- This obligation extends to **third-party service providers** handling personal data on behalf of the entity

### NDB Scheme (Part IIIC)
- Mandatory notification when an eligible data breach occurs
- Applies to breaches through third-party processors as well as direct breaches

## Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)

The 13 APPs govern how personal information is handled across its lifecycle — these create direct obligations that organisations must flow down to their service providers contractually.

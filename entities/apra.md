# APRA — Australian Prudential Regulation Authority

## Overview

APRA is the Australian government body responsible for prudential regulation of banks, credit unions, building societies, insurers, and superannuation funds. It was established in 1998 under the Australian Prudential Regulation Authority Act 1998.

## Key Role in Supply Chain / TPRM

APRA is the most direct driver of TPRM adoption in the Australian financial sector through [[apra-cps-230]], effective 1 July 2025. This standard:

- Mandates Board-approved service provider management policies
- Requires identification and monitoring of **critical service providers**
- Requires exit/transition provisions in all material service provider contracts
- Mandates pre-contractual risk assessments
- Sets thresholds that effectively require TPRM programs for all APRA-regulated entities

## Supervised Entities

- **ADIs** — banks, building societies, credit unions (~130 entities)
- **Insurers** — general, life, and private health (~350 entities)
- **Superannuation** — RSE licensees (~160 entities)

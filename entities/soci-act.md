# SOCI Act — Security of Critical Infrastructure Act 2018

## Overview

The Security of Critical Infrastructure Act 2018 (SOCI Act) regulates critical infrastructure across 11 sectors. Significantly expanded in 2022-2023 via the SOCI Amendment Act 2022. Administered by [[cisc]].

## Effective Date

The 2022 amendments came into effect progressively. Risk management program obligations under Part 2 became mandatory for most sectors by late 2023/early 2024.

## Applicability — 11 Sectors

1. Communications
2. Financial services and markets
3. Data storage or processing
4. Defence industry
5. Higher education and research
6. Energy
7. Food and grocery
8. Healthcare and medical
9. Space technology
10. Transport
11. Water and sewerage

## Key Obligations

### All Critical Infrastructure Entities
1. **Register** — provide ownership and operational information to CISC
2. **Report cyber incidents** — notify ACSC of incidents impacting essential services
3. **Risk management program** — written program covering physical, personnel, supply chain, and cyber/information security risks

### Systems of National Significance (SoNS)
Additional Enhanced Cyber Security Obligations:
- Cyber security incident response plans
- Cyber security exercises
- Vulnerability assessments
- System information sharing with ACSC

## Supply Chain Requirements

The 2022 amendments explicitly added supply chain as a standalone risk domain. Entities must assess:
- Foreign involvement in supply chains
- OT and IT component security from third-party providers
- Fourth-party (sub-processor) risks

## Compliance Posture

| Period | Approach |
|--------|---------|
| 2023-24 | Education, awareness, trial audits |
| 2024-25 | Education + enforcement activities |

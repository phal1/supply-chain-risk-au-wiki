# ASIC — Australian Securities and Investments Commission

## Overview

ASIC is Australia's integrated corporate, financial services, and markets regulator. It regulates companies, financial services providers, credit intermediaries, and market infrastructure operators under the Corporations Act 2001 and other legislation.

## Role in Supply Chain / Operational Risk

While [[apra]] regulates the prudential (safety and soundness) side of banking and insurance, ASIC focuses on market integrity, consumer protection, and corporate governance. ASIC's interest in operational risk and supply chain is more indirect:

### ASIC Expectations for Market Intermediaries

ASIC's supervision priorities for market intermediaries (brokers, clearing houses, etc.) include:
- Operational resilience frameworks
- Technology risk management
- Business continuity arrangements

### Key Issues Outlook 2025

ASIC's 2025 Key Issues Outlook flagged:
- Operational risk management
- Technology and cyber resilience
- Third-party dependencies in financial markets

### Regulatory Tracker 2025

ASIC publishes a regulatory tracker showing new and updated guidance. The 2025 edition includes updates to resilience guidance, reducing duplication with APRA standards while maintaining market-specific expectations.

## ASIC vs APRA in TPRM Context

| | APRA | ASIC |
|--|------|------|
| Focus | Prudential (safety/soundness) | Market integrity/consumer protection |
| TPRM Mandate | CPS 230 (direct mandate) | Expectations, not explicit standard |
| Scope | Banks, insurers, super | Markets, corps, financial services |

Many entities are dual-regulated (e.g., banks are APRA-supervised but also subject to ASIC market rules).

# IIA New Third Party Requirements (September 2025)

**Source:** InConsult / Institute of Internal Auditors (IIA)  
**Published:** September 2025  
**Effective Date:** September 15th, 2026

## Overview

On 15 September 2025, the IIA issued new **Topical Requirements** for third party management auditing. This is a new **mandatory element** of the IIA International Professional Practices Framework (IPPF).

## Key Statistic

> **~90% of Australian businesses lack a formal risk management process** (Vero Insurance SME Insurance Index 2024/2025)

> **81-82% never or rarely conduct risk analyses when required**

## Seven Key Challenges Identified

### 1. Documentation and Evidence Requirements
- Auditors must document evidence of assessment of formally structured frameworks
- ASA 530 requires confidence of **90-95% or higher** for control adequacy
- Key controls: deviation must be as low as **0-5%**

### 2. Governance Gaps in Oversight
- Most organisations delegate third-party risk to Procurement and/or IT
- Many lack resources dedicated to third-party management
- Delegation to IT roles (e.g., Cyber Security Lead) introduces concerns — these roles often lack required Risk Management knowledge

### 3. Consistent Risk Management Throughout Lifecycle
- Formal, functional enterprise risk management framework covering: Selection → Onboarding → Monitoring → Offboarding
- ~90% of Australian businesses lack this

### 4. Ongoing Monitoring
- "Set and forget" contracts no longer acceptable
- Even multi-year contracts require: performance assessment, compliance assessment, cyber control assessment
- **71%** of companies that do assess third parties do not include security practices in assessment

### 5. Regulatory Alignment
Requirements reference compliance with:
- Privacy Act
- APRA CPS 230 (pre-existing contracts delayed to **July 2026** for non-SFIs)
- APRA CPS 234 (Information Security)
- SOCI Act
- Modern Slavery

### 6. Strain on Smaller Organisations
- Large corporations will absorb changes easily
- Local Government councils, NFPs, small businesses will face significant new audit and compliance focus

### 7. Cultural Resistance
- Third-party management is NOT just a procurement task
- Requires enterprise-level strategy delivering: cost savings, efficiencies, lower insurance premiums, greater coverage

## Compliance Timeline Note
- IIA Topical Requirements effective: **September 15th, 2026**
- APRA CPS 230 pre-existing contracts deadline: **1 July 2026**

# Supply Chain Third-Party Risk Escalates (AICD)

**Source:** Australian Institute of Company Directors (AICD)  
**Date:** 11 April 2025  
**Author:** Megan Bonny

## Key Statistics from ASIC Survey

| Metric | Percentage |
|--------|------------|
| Organisations that do NOT manage third-party/supply chain risk | **44%** |
| Small organisations with minimal/no third-party risk capabilities | **69%** |
| Organisations that don't test cybersecurity incident responses of critical suppliers | **Nearly 6 in 10 (~58%)** |

## Regulatory Landscape

### ASIC Report 776: Spotlight on Cyber
Requires third-party risk assessments, clear contractual obligations, mapping of critical business services and dependencies.

### National Cyber Security Strategy 2023–2030
Shifts cyber from technical issue to whole-of-nation concern.

### Cyber Security Act 2024
Passed November 2024; extends cybersecurity rules to managing risk with third-party vendors and suppliers.

## AI Privacy Risk in Supply Chains

> "If an organisation's third-party AI provider fails to comply with the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs), the organisation itself may be in breach." — Dan Pearce, General Counsel, Holding Redlich

OAIC has flagged privacy risks where personal data is used to train AI models without individuals' knowledge or consent.

## Threat Landscape Statistics

- **Generative AI-driven attacks (vishing):** surged 442% between H1 and H2 2024
- **China-Nexus intrusions** in financial services, media, manufacturing: increased 200-300% vs prior years
- **Australian data breaches H1 2024:** 527 notifications (9% increase over prior period)

## Board Recommendations

1. Monitor third-party vendors continuously
2. Ensure third parties have modern security solutions
3. Push for details about third-party cybersecurity and compliance
4. Follow-up on reviews and identify how to improve resilience
5. Establish robust metrics including incident response testing

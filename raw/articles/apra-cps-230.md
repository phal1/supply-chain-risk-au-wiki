# APRA CPS 230 — Operational Risk Management

**Source:** APRA Prudential Standard CPS 230  
**Effective Date:** 1 July 2025  
**Supersedes:** CPS 231 Outsourcing, CPS 232 Business Continuity Management, SPS 231, SPS 232, HPS 231

## Overview

CPS 230 consolidates and replaces five prior APRA prudential standards relating to outsourcing and business continuity. It applies to all APRA-regulated entities: ADIs (banks), general insurers, life insurers, private health insurers, and RSE licensees (superannuation).

The standard has three core principles:
1. Effectively manage operational risks
2. Maintain critical operations within tolerance through severe disruptions
3. Cannot rely on service providers unless able to meet prudential obligations in full

## Key Requirements

### Service Provider Management

- Entities must have a **service provider management policy** approved by the Board
- **Critical service providers** must be identified and assessed for disruption risk
- Contracts must include **exit and termination provisions** enabling timely transition
- Pre-contractual risk assessments required before engaging material service providers
- Ongoing monitoring of service provider performance and risk

### Business Continuity

- **Critical operations** must be identified (payments, clearing, custody for banks; claims processing for insurers; customer enquiries for all)
- **Tolerance levels** must be set: maximum tolerable disruption period, maximum data loss, minimum service levels under alternative arrangements
- **BCPs must be tested** with severe but plausible scenarios
- BCPs must be **consistent with recovery and exit planning** — no conflicts

### Operational Risk Framework

- Maintain an **operational risk profile assessment**
- Defined **risk appetite** with indicators, limits, and tolerance levels
- **Internal controls** designed and operating effectively
- Regular **monitoring, analysis, and reporting** with escalation procedures
- **Scenario analysis** for severe operational risk events

## APRA Intervention Powers

Where operational risk management is materially weak, APRA may:
- Require independent review
- Require remediation program
- Require additional capital
- Impose licence conditions
- Take other supervisory actions

## Pre-Existing Contracts

Requirements apply from the earlier of: next contract renewal date OR 1 July 2026.

# Security of Critical Infrastructure Act 2018 (SOCI Act)

**Source:** Department of Home Affairs / CISC  
**Current Version:** Amended 2022-2023 (SOCI Amendment Act 2022)  
**Applicability:** 11 critical infrastructure sectors

## Overview

The SOCI Act establishes positive security obligations for owners and operators of critical infrastructure assets in Australia. The Act was significantly expanded in 2022-2023 to address evolving threats, particularly cyber risks to supply chains.

## 11 Covered Sectors

1. Communications
2. Financial services and markets
3. Data storage or processing
4. Defence industry
5. Higher education and research
6. Energy
7. Food and grocery
8. Healthcare and medical
9. Space technology
10. Transport
11. Water and sewerage

## Positive Security Obligations (All Assets)

1. **Register information** — provide operational and ownership information to the Register of Critical Infrastructure Assets
2. **Report cyber incidents** — notify the Australian Cyber Security Centre (ACSC) of cyber incidents impacting delivery of essential services
3. **Risk management program** — adopt, maintain, and comply with a written risk management program covering:
   - Physical security
   - Personnel security
   - Supply chain security
   - **Cyber and information security** (added in 2022 amendments)

## Enhanced Cyber Security Obligations (SoNS)

Systems of National Significance (SoNS) assets face four additional obligations:
1. Cyber security incident response plans
2. Cyber security exercises
3. Vulnerability assessments
4. Providing system information to ACSC for near real-time threat picture

## Supply Chain Risk

The Act explicitly acknowledges supply chain risks as a core hazard area. The 2022 amendments added supply chain security as a standalone obligation in the risk management program requirements. Entities must assess:
- Foreign involvement in supply chains
- OT/IT component security
- Third-party service provider risks

## Compliance Timeline

| Period | Approach |
|--------|---------|
| 2023–24 | Education and awareness; limited trial audits |
| 2024–25 | Balanced education + enforcement |

## Government Assistance

The Act enables government support for critical infrastructure entities during incidents causing serious harm to Australia's prosperity, national security, or defence.

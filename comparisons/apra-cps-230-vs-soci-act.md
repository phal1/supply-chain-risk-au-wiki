# APRA CPS 230 vs SOCI Act — Scope Comparison

## Overview

APRA CPS 230 and the SOCI Act both address third-party and supply chain risk, but they have different scopes, regulators, and applicability. Many entities are subject to both.

## Key Comparison

| Dimension | APRA CPS 230 | SOCI Act |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Regulator** | APRA | CISC (Critical Infrastructure Commission) |
| **Sector** | Financial services, insurance, superannuation | All 11 critical infrastructure sectors |
| **Scope** | APRA-regulated entities (~640) | All critical infrastructure entities |
| **Supply Chain Focus** | Service provider management, critical providers | Supply chain as standalone risk domain |
| **Effective Date** | 1 July 2025 | Parts effective from 2023-24 |
| **Pre-existing Contracts** | 1 July 2026 (or next renewal) | Various |
| **Key Driver** | Operational resilience | National security, critical infrastructure |
| **Cyber Requirements** | Via CPS 234 | Explicitly in risk management program |

## What They Share

- Both require written risk management programs
- Both require identification of critical dependencies
- Both require supply chain/sub-processor consideration
- Both require Board-level oversight
- Both are driving TPRM program adoption

## What Each Covers Uniquely

### CPS 230
- Exit/transition provisions in contracts
- BCP testing with severe but plausible scenarios
- Specific critical operations per entity type (payments, clearing, claims processing)
- Pre-contractual risk assessments

### SOCI Act
- Foreign involvement in supply chains explicitly assessed
- SoNS assets with Enhanced Cyber Security Obligations
- Direct ACSC cyber incident reporting requirement
- 11 sector scope beyond financial services

## Entities Subject to Both

Banks and insurers are typically subject to both:
- APRA CPS 230 as APRA-regulated entities
- SOCI Act as financial services and markets sector entities

## Key Regulatory Drivers

1. **CPS 230 (1 July 2025)** — ~640 APRA-regulated entities must have service provider management frameworks
2. **SOCI Act** — 11 critical infrastructure sectors with supply chain risk program requirements
3. **NDB Scheme** — OAIC reporting ~530-595 breaches per half-year, third-party breaches consistently highlighted
4. **Privacy Act reforms** — ongoing, may introduce stricter third-party data handling
5. **Cyber Security Act 2024** — extends cyber rules to third-party vendors

## Competitive Dynamics

### Key Differentiators in Australian Market

1. **APRA/SOCI native compliance** — platforms with pre-built APRA and SOCI templates and reporting get traction faster
2. **Fourth-party visibility** — given 82% don't assess beyond Tier 1, fourth-party discovery is a key differentiator (Black Kite, TPRM360, Interos lead here)
3. **Continuous vs periodic** — shift from annual assessments to continuous monitoring (BitSight, UpGuard, Black Kite)
4. **Integration with existing stack** — ServiceNow TPRM wins where enterprises already have ServiceNow
5. **Australian data residency** — vendors with local data centres get preference in financial services and government

### Competitive Threats / Substitutes

- **Manual processes / spreadsheets** — still prevalent in SMEs; threat until compliance deadline
- **Generalist GRC tools** — RSA Archer, Diligent position as TPRM-capable
- **In-house build** — large banks sometimes build bespoke TPRM systems

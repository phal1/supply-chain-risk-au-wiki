## Key Regulatory Drivers in Australia

1. **CPS 230 (1 July 2025)** — All APRA-regulated entities must have service provider management frameworks. This affects ~640 entities across banking, insurance, and superannuation.

2. **SOCI Act (as amended 2022)** — 11 critical infrastructure sectors must implement supply chain risk management programs. CISC compliance enforcement stepped up in 2024-25.

3. **NDB Scheme** — OAIC reporting shows ~530-595 data breach notifications per half-year, with third-party breaches a recurring theme in OAIC guidance.

4. **Privacy Act reforms** — Ongoing reform discussions may introduce stricter third-party data handling requirements.

## Problem Statement (Market Opportunity)

- ASIC survey: **44% of Australian organisations manage third-party risk poorly**
- McGrathNicol/YouGov: **82% do not extend assessments beyond Tier 1 suppliers** — massive fourth-party gap
- Only **18% have mature fourth-party visibility** (estimated from vendor surveys)

## Market Trends

1. **Continuous monitoring replacing point-in-time assessments** — driven by speed of third-party change
2. **Automated vendor tiering** — based on criticality scoring
3. **Fourth-party/sub-processor discovery** — automated scanning for sub-processors
4. **Regulatory convergence** — CPS 230, SOCI, and Privacy Act driving uniform TPRM requirements
5. **Board-level visibility** — CPS 230 mandates Board reporting on service provider risk

# Supply Chain Risk — Concept

## Definition

Supply chain risk is the potential for disruption, failure, or compromise at any point in an organisation's supply chain — from raw materials through manufacturing, distribution, and delivery to end customers. In the TPRM context, it encompasses risks arising from third-party providers and their sub-processors.

## Risk Vectors

### Concentration Risk
Over-reliance on single or few providers for critical goods/services. A single provider failure cascades across all dependent operations.

### Geopolitical Risk
Foreign involvement in supply chains, particularly for critical infrastructure. Relevant to [[soci-act]] requirements.

### Cyber/Information Security Risk
Third-party systems as attack vectors. The 2022 [[soci-act]] amendments explicitly added cyber and information security as a supply chain hazard domain.

### Operational Disruption Risk
Provider outages, natural disasters, financial distress, or other events that interrupt service delivery. [[apra-cps-230]] specifically addresses this.

### Regulatory/Compliance Risk
Non-compliance by a supplier creates liability for the principal organisation (e.g., [[oaic-notifiable-data-breaches]]).

### Data Sovereignty Risk
Data stored or processed by third parties in jurisdictions with different legal requirements.

## Fourth Party Risk (Nth-Party Risk)

The risk posed by sub-processors and subcontractors beyond direct third-party relationships. Many organisations have poor visibility beyond Tier 1:
- 82% of Australian companies do not extend risk assessments beyond Tier 1 suppliers (McGrathNicol/YouGov, Aug 2024)

## Supply Chain Risk in Australia

### Regulatory Drivers
- **SOCI Act** — Part 2 requires written risk management programs covering supply chain
- **CPS 230** — requires assessment of service provider concentration and fourth-party risks
- **Privacy Act** — entities responsible for breaches through any processor

### Current Challenges
- Limited visibility beyond Tier 1 suppliers
- Increasing outsourcing of critical functions
- Digital transformation expanding attack surface
- Global supply chain disruptions (pandemic, geopolitical)
